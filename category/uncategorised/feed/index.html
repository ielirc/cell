“A judge must interpret statutes as written. And a judge must interpret the Constitution as written, informed by history and tradition and precedent.”

Brett Kavanaugh, Associate Justice of Supreme Court of India

In a world characterized by global interdependence and economic integration, understanding the nuances of international trade agreements is of paramount importance. The International Economic Law and International Relations Cell (IELIRC) is proud to announce its inaugural event – the 1st Declamation Competition, scheduled for the 21st of September, 2023. This competition seeks to explore the intriguing theme, “India and its Bargaining Power under the Free Trade Agreement: Understanding the Influence of Non-Tariff Barriers in International Trade.” As the global economy continues to evolve, comprehending the role of non-tariff barriers in shaping trade dynamics is crucial. In this blog, we delve into the significance of this competition and why it matters in today’s complex world of international economics.

According to Gray, “Precedent covers everything said or done, which furnishes a rule for subsequent practice.” According to Salmond, “In a loose sense, it includes merely reported case law which may be cited and followed by courts.” In general judicial sense, it means the guidance or authority of past decisions for future cases. Only such decisions as lay down some new rule or principle are called judicial precedents.

The Importance of International Trade Agreements

The theme of the 1st Declamation Competition, “India and its Bargaining Power under the Free Trade Agreement: Understanding the Influence of Non-Tariff Barriers in International Trade,” offers a unique opportunity to dissect India’s role in international trade agreements. It highlights the significance of non-tariff barriers (NTBs), which are increasingly becoming essential tools in shaping trade dynamics.

Non-tariff barriers encompass a wide range of measures, including technical regulations, sanitary and phytosanitary standards, licensing procedures, and quotas, among others. These barriers often have a substantial impact on international trade, as they can hinder or facilitate the movement of goods and services. Understanding how India navigates these barriers and leverages its bargaining power is essential to analyze its position in the global trade landscape.

Competition Format

The 1st Declamation Competition is designed to provide a platform for students, scholars, and practitioners interested in international economics and relations to showcase their research and analytical skills. Participants are encouraged to present their insights and perspectives on India’s role in FTAs, with a particular focus on non-tariff barriers.

The competition format includes declamations (speeches) where participants will present their well-researched arguments and viewpoints. These speeches will be judged based on their content, clarity, coherence, and persuasiveness. It is an excellent opportunity for participants to hone their public speaking and research skills while delving into the intricate world of international trade.

Conclusion

The 1st Declamation Competition by the International Economic Law and International Relations Cell promises to be a stimulating and informative event. By addressing the theme of India’s bargaining power under FTAs and the influence of non-tariff barriers, this competition encourages participants to explore the dynamic world of international trade. It is a platform where young minds can shine, research can flourish, and ideas can be exchanged, contributing to the ever-evolving field of international economics and relations. Don’t miss this chance to be part of an intellectually enriching experience on the 21st of September, 2023.
